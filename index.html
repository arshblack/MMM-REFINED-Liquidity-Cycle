The Methodology · Three Pillars
I
Structure

Read the Market's Blueprint

Identify Higher Highs / Higher Lows for bullish and Lower Highs / Lower Lows for bearish. Structure tells you where price wants to go.

  • Break of Structure (BOS)
  • Change of Character (CHoCH)
  • Order Blocks & Fair Value Gaps
Tap to reveal EA logic →
EA · Pillar I
Structure = Bias
IF (Structure == HH/HL) → BIAS = BULLISH IF (Structure == LH/LL) → BIAS = BEARISH CHoCH → BOS → EXECUTE_SIGNAL()

The EA reads H1 structure first on every bar. No trade is considered until bias is confirmed. CHoCH is the warning; BOS is the trigger. Without structure alignment, the signal never fires.

II
Liquidity

Follow the Smart Money

Banks need liquidity to fill orders. They engineer moves to sweep stop losses before the real move. Spot the trap before you become it.

  • Equal Highs / Lows = Targets
  • Stop Hunt Mechanics
  • Liquidity Sweep → Reversal
Tap to reveal EA logic →
EA · Pillar II
Sweep = Confidence
EQH_DETECTED → await sweep IF (EQH_SWEEP confirmed) CONFIDENCE++ STOP_HUNT pattern → flag entry

Every EA signal requires a confirmed EQH or EQL sweep. The sweep candle must close back inside the range — the wick is the hunt. This single filter eliminates the majority of false signals.

III
Timing

Strike at the Right Moment

The best setups happen during London and NY sessions. Session timing separates professional discretion from gambling.

  • London Open: 07:00 GMT
  • New York Open: 12:00 GMT
  • Killzone Overlap: 12:00–15:00
Tap to reveal EA logic →
EA · Pillar III
Session = Filter
SESSION_FILTER = London + NY_KZ IF current_time NOT IN filter: SKIP_SIGNAL() // always KILLZONE = 12:00–15:00 GMT

The EA hard-blocks all signals outside London open and the NY killzone. 28% of retail entries fire off-session and lose twice as often. The session filter alone accounts for a significant portion of the EA's edge.
